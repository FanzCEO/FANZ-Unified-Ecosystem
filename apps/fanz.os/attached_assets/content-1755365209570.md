• Case Law: Specht v. Netscape Communications Corp., 306 F.3d 17 (2d Cir. 2002)

confirmed that users must actively accept terms for an agreement to be binding.

**B. Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (E-SIGN Act) (15 U.S.C. Chapter 96)**

• This U.S. federal law gives legal validity to electronic contracts and signatures.

• The **E-SIGN Act** ensures that digital consent **(clicking ‘I Agree’) is legally binding, just like a physical signature.**

**C. Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA)**

• This act, adopted by **Wyoming** and 46 other U.S. states, reinforces the legality of electronic contracts.

• UETA ensures that contracts signed online hold the same enforceability as paper contracts.

**D. General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – Article 6(1)(b)**

• The GDPR states that a platform may process user data if it is necessary to fulfill a contract.

• By signing the User Agreement, users consent to the collection, processing, and retention of their data for legal and operational purposes.

**2.1 PROHIBITED CONTENT POLICY**

**2.1.1 Legal Basis for Content Moderation**

**Content moderation is legally required under:**

• 18 U.S.C. § 2252A (U.S. Federal Law on CSAM)

• 47 U.S.C. § 230 (Communications Decency Act) – Platform liability protection

• U.S. Animal Crush Video Prohibition Act (18 U.S.C. § 48) – Bestiality prohibition

• EU Digital Services Act (DSA) 2022/2065 – Mandatory content removal deadlines

**2.1.2 Categories of Prohibited Content**

**The following content is strictly prohibited and subject to immediate removal and legal action:**

**A. Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)**

• Defined under 18 U.S.C. § 2256(8) as any visual depiction of sexually explicit conduct involving minors.

• Mandatory reporting to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) under 18 U.S.C. § 2258A.

• Immediate account termination and law enforcement notification required.

**B. Bestiality & Animal Cruelty**

• Criminalized under the U.S. Animal Crush Video Prohibition Act (18 U.S.C. § 48).

• Includes real or simulated depictions of sexual acts with animals.

• Immediate content removal and account suspension.

**C. Non-Consensual Intimate Media (Revenge Porn)**

• Defined as the distribution of sexually explicit content without consent.

• Illegal under various state revenge porn laws and GDPR Article 9 (sensitive data processing restrictions).

• Content is removed within 24 hours of a valid report.

• Repeat violations result in permanent bans and potential civil liability.

**D. Human Trafficking, Sexual Exploitation & Coerced Content**

• Criminal under the Trafficking Victims Protection Act (22 U.S.C. § 7102).

• Includes any forced or non-consensual sexual activity recorded or distributed.

• Immediate account termination, content takedown, and law enforcement escalation.

**E. Hate Speech, Terrorist Content, and Violent Extremism**

• Hate speech is defined under UN Human Rights Council Resolution 16/18.

• Terrorist content is defined under the U.S. PATRIOT Act and EU Terrorist Content Regulation 2021/784.

• Content removal within 24 hours under the EU Digital Services Act (DSA) 2022/2065.

**F. Copyright Infringement & Unauthorized Content Distribution**

• Covered under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) (17 U.S.C. § 512).

• Includes unauthorized uploads of copyrighted movies, music, and books.

• DMCA takedown process applies (detailed in Section 2.2).

**G. Fraud, Scams & Malicious Content**

• Includes phishing scams, identity theft, and fraudulent financial transactions.

• Violates the U.S. Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030).

• Account suspension and potential referral to financial crime authorities.

**5.1 U.S. LAWS & REGULATIONS**

**5.1.1 Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) (17 U.S.C. § 512)**

The DMCA governs copyright protection for digital content and provides Safe Harbor protections for online platforms.

**A. DMCA Safe Harbor Provisions (17 U.S.C. § 512)**

**To avoid liability for copyright infringement,** **FUN must:**

✔ Have a registered DMCA Agent to receive complaints.

✔ Follow a notice-and-takedown procedure when a copyright holder submits a complaint.

✔ Allow users to submit counter-notifications if their content was removed unfairly.

✔Terminate repeat infringers (users with 3+ DMCA violations).

**C.** **DMCA Compliance Steps for FUN**

1.DMCA complaints must be reviewed within 24 hours.

2.Infringing content must be removed immediately upon verification.

3.If the user files a counter-notification, the copyright holder has 14 days to file a lawsuit.

4.Repeat violators must be permanently banned from FUN platforms.

**D. Legal Consequences for Non-Compliance**

•If FUN fails to follow DMCA takedown rules, it loses Safe Harbor protection and may be held liable for copyright infringement.

•Statutory Damages: Up to $150,000 per instance of willful infringement (17 U.S.C. § 504).

**5.1.2 Communications Decency Act (CDA) – Section 230 (47 U.S.C. § 230)**

**The****CDA Section 230****protects****online platforms from liability for user-generated content****.**

**A. Platform Immunity Under Section 230**

**✔FUN is NOT legally responsible for content uploaded by users.**

**✔FUN CAN remove harmful content (e.g., CSAM, hate speech, copyright violations) without losing immunity.**

**B. Exceptions Where FUN May Be Liable**

❌ If FUN knowingly allows illegal content (e.g., CSAM, human trafficking) to remain on its platforms.

❌ If FUN publishes its own illegal content.

**C. Enforcement Steps for FUN**

✔Strict content moderation policies must be in place.

✔CSAM & human trafficking content must be reported within 24 hours under U.S. federal law.

✔Legal compliance logs must be maintained to prove FUN acted in good faith.

**5.1.3 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100 – 1798.199)**

**The****CCPA****protects****California residents’ data privacy rights****.**

**A. User Rights Under CCPA**

✔**Right to Know:** Users can request a copy of their stored data.

✔**Right to Delete:** Users can request data deletion (except when legal holds apply).

✔**Right to Opt-Out:** Users can stop FUN from selling their personal data.

**B. FUN’s Obligations Under CCPA**

✔FUN must provide a privacy notice explaining what data is collected.

✔Users must be able to request data deletion via a simple process.

✔Data access and deletion requests must be processed within 45 days.

**C. Legal Consequences for Non-Compliance**

**•** **Fines:** Up to $7,500 per intentional violation.

**•** **Class-Action Lawsuits:** Users can sue for data breaches caused by FUN’s negligence.

**5.1.4 Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) (15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506)**

**The** **COPPA** **law applies to any platform that collects data from** **children under 13 years old** **.**

**A. FUN’s Compliance Requirements**

✔FUN must NOT allow minors under 18 to register.

✔If FUN collects data from minors, parental consent is required.

**B. Legal Consequences for Non-Compliance**

**•** **Fines:** Up to $43,280 per violation.

**•** **Regulatory Investigations:** The FTC can conduct audits on FUN’s data practices.

**5.2 EUROPEAN UNION LAWS & REGULATIONS**

**5.2.1 General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU Regulation 2016/679)**

**The****GDPR****is the most****comprehensive data protection law in the world****, covering****all European users.**

A **. FUN’s Obligations Under GDPR**

✔ **Article 5:** Data must be processed lawfully, transparently, and securely.

✔**Article 6:** FUN must have a legal basis for processing user data (e.g., consent, contract fulfillment).

✔**Article 17:**Users have the Right to Be Forgotten (FUN must delete user data upon request, unless legal retention laws apply).

**B. Legal Consequences for Non-Compliance**

• **Fines:** Up to €20 million or 4%of annual global revenue (whichever is higher).

•**Data Processing Bans:** The EU can block FUN from operating in Europe.

**5.2.2 EU Digital Services Act (DSA) (Regulation 2022/2065)**

**The****DSA****requires****faster content removal and platform accountability****.**

**A. Key Compliance Rules for FUN**

**✔** **Illegal content must be removed within 24 hours.**

**✔** **FUN must publish transparency reports on moderation decisions.**

**B. Legal Consequences for Non-Compliance**

•Fines: Up to 6% of global revenue.

•Service Bans: The EU can suspend FUN in all EU member states.
